# Belirsiz Geçmiş Zaman

In Turkish there is an explicit past tense(belirtili geçmiş zaman) and narrated past tense(belirsiz geçmiş zaman).

You can read about <CustomLink name="explicit simple past🔗" url="https://www.pickturkish.com/posts/a1/simple-past-belirtili" /> (-dı, -di, -tı, -ti).

In this article we are looking at implicit(narrated) simple past.

It is used to express things in the past we know have happened but haven’t **witnessed** it. It is used for things we **don’t** have **first hand evidence**.

The suffix structure is “-m**I**ş”, where **I** → i, ı, u, ü depending on the last vowel according to <CustomLink name="vowel harmony rule🔗." url="https://www.pickturkish.com/posts/a1/vowel-harmony"  />

<aside>
📌 **-mIş** doesn’t necessarily imply past tense. It is usually used in day to day language when people realize somethings or find things different than they expected.

For example,

When you taste a food for the first time and enjoy it but you didn’t expect to. You might say “Hmm güzel**miş**.”

Or

When you realize that It was a bad idea to call you ex, you could say “Onu aramak hatay**mış**”

</aside>

---

♟️ If the last letter of the word a **noun** or an **adjective** is a **vowel,** ‘<span className="text-orange-500 font-bold">y</span>’ comes in between as a separator.

♟️ There is no irregular verb in Turkish. The conversion to past tense by adding -**mIs** suffix applies universally to all roots of a verb.

♟️ Also keep in mind that if you hear a lot **-mış** being used in a conversation there is a good chance that it is a gossip.

♟️ Stories are also narrated using the -**mış** past tense suffix. That is why they start with “Bir varmış bir yokmuş”
