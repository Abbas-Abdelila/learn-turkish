## Simple past tense -DI and -DU (Belirtili Geçmiş Zaman)
---
Simple past tense in Turkish is actually very simple :).

The suffix that is used in the past tense of nouns, verbs or an adjectives is -**DI** or -**DU**.
    
    - **D** → `d` or `t` (The **f-s-t-k-ç-ş-h-p** rule)

    - **I**   → `ı` or `i` (Vowel harmony i.e ease of pronunciation)   

    - **U**  → `u` or `ü` (Vowel harmony i.e ease of pronunciation)

📝 *The ‘**d**’ becomes → ‘**t**’  when the last letter is one of the **f-s-t-k-ç-ş-h-p**.*

### Examples

<p className="text-center text-red-500 text-2xl ">1. Verbs</p>
<span className="text-blue-500 font-semibold"></span>

| Turkish Verb  | English Verb  | Past Tense (3rd Person Singular) | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| <span className="text-green-600">Gel</span><span className="text-yellow-500">mek</span> | To come | <span className="text-green-600">Ge<span className="text-blue-600 font-semibold">l</span></span>**di** | came |
| <span className="text-green-600">Git</span><span className="text-yellow-500">mek</span> | To go | <span className="text-green-600">Gi<span className="text-blue-600 font-semibold">t</span></span>**ti** | went |
| <span className="text-green-600">Zıpla</span><span className="text-yellow-500">mak</span> | To jump | <span className="text-green-600">Zıpl<span className="text-blue-600 font-semibold">a</span></span>**dı** | jumped |
| <span className="text-green-600">Yap</span><span className="text-yellow-500">mak</span> | To do | <span className="text-green-600">Ya<span className="text-blue-600 font-semibold">p</span></span>**tı** | did |
| <span className="text-green-600">Uyu</span><span className="text-yellow-500">mak</span> | To sleep | <span className="text-green-600">Uy<span className="text-blue-600 font-semibold">u</span></span>**du** | slept |
| <span className="text-green-600">Gül</span><span className="text-yellow-500">mek</span> | To laugh | <span className="text-green-600">Gü<span className="text-blue-600 font-semibold">l</span></span>**dü** | laughed |

    - 🟩 - root of verb
    - 🟨 - indicator of verb (All verbs in Turkish end with `mak` or `mek`)
    - 🟥 - past tense suffix
    - 🟦 - last letter of root

<p className="text-center text-red-500 text-2xl ">2. Adjectives</p>

| Turkish Adjective | English Adjective | Past Tense (3rd Person Singular) | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Büyük | Big  | Büyü<span className="text-blue-500 font-semibold">k</span>**tü** | It was big |
| Küçük | Small | Küçü<span className="text-blue-500 font-semibold">k</span>**tü** | It was small |
| Yeni | New  | Yen<span className="text-blue-500 font-semibold">i</span><span className="text-orange-500 font-semibold">y</span>**di** | It was new |
| Eski | Old | Esk<span className="text-blue-500 font-semibold">i</span><span className="text-orange-500 font-semibold">y</span>**di** | It was old |
| Güzel | Beautiful | Güze<span className="text-blue-500 font-semibold">l</span>**di** | It was beautiful |
| İyi | Good | İy<span className="text-blue-500 font-semibold">i</span><span className="text-orange-500 font-semibold">y</span>**di** | It was good |

    - 🟦 - last letter of word
    - 🟧 - separator 'y' between two vowels (only in noun and adjective), **not** in verbs ❌. 
    - 🟥 - past tense suffix

<p className="text-center text-red-500 text-2xl ">3. Nouns</p>

| Turkish Noun | English Noun | Past Tense (3rd Person Singular) | English Translation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Masa | Table | …güzel masa<span className="text-orange-500 font-semibold">y</span>**dı**. | It was beautiful table |
| Resim | Picture | …büyük resim**di**. | It was big picture |
| Arkadaş | Friend  | …samimi arkadaş**tı**. | He/she was sincere friend  |
| Şehir | City  | …büyük şehir**di**. | It was big city |
| Yemek | Food | …lezzetli yemek**ti**. | It was delicious food |
| Müzik | Music | ...harika müzik**ti**. | It was great music |

---

♟️ If the last letter of the word a **noun** or an **adjective** is a **vowel,** ‘<span className="text-orange-500 font-semibold">y</span>’ comes in between as a separator.

♟️ There is also a non suffix way of expressing past tense by using the word **idi** after a noun and adjective.
    - ♟️ Example would be güzel**di** → güzel **idi**, durust**tu** → durust **idi**.
        - In this case **no need** to consider `vowel harmony` or `f-s-t-k-ç-ş-h-p` rules, just add **idi** to any noun or adjective.
